## Four benchmark families

| Example | Crystal / load | Mobility | Main question |
|---------|----------------|----------|---------------|
| **1 — glissile loops** | BCC & FCC; constant stress on (001) / (111) | `BCC_Linear`, `BCC_glide`, `FCC_linear` | Does a shear loop expand in its glide plane? |
| **2 — Frank–Read** | BCC source; $\sigma_{zz}=5\times10^8\,\mathrm{Pa}$ | `BCC_glide` + FMM elasticity | Does a pinned segment bow out and multiply? |
| **3 — strain harden Cu** | 15 µm FCC Cu; $\dot\varepsilon=10^3\,\mathrm{s^{-1}}$ along [001] | `FCC_0` | Stress–strain & network evolution (OpenDiS analogue) |
| **4 — strain harden Al** | Bulk FCC Al; $\dot\varepsilon=10^4\,\mathrm{s^{-1}}$ along [100] | `FCC_linear` | Same, with cross-slip enabled |

All cases use full elastic segment–segment forces (`elasticinteraction = 1`) unless noted.

---

## 1 — Glissile loops: setup

- **Geometry:** planar loop from `shearloopBCC_LinCS`, rotated to (001) or (111); 4 µm box.
- **Loading:** constant Voigt stress (rotated with the plane); no strain-rate control.
- **Mobility families:**
  - `BCC_Linear` — reference linear drag ($\mathrm{MobEdge}/\mathrm{MobScrew}=100$).
  - `BCC_glide` — tensor glide + climb; `MobClimb` tuned per plane.
  - `FCC_linear` — isotropic FCC drag for comparison.

---

## 1 — (001) `BCC_glide`: loop expands ~1.6×

![(001) BCC_glide: cycle 200 vs 4000](../1_glissile_loops/001_BCC_glide/output/first_vs_final.png)

Mobility fix for zero-component Burgers vectors + $MobClimb=10^{-3}$: density $1.19\to1.93\times10^{12}\,\mathrm{m^{-2}}$; visible in-plane growth (001 view).

---

## 1 — (111) `BCC_glide`: stronger expansion (~2.1×)

![(111) BCC_glide: cycle 200 vs 4000](../1_glissile_loops/111_BCC_glide/output/first_vs_final.png)

(111) plane needs slightly higher climb mobility ($MobClimb=3\times10^{-3}$): density $1.19\to2.53\times10^{12}\,\mathrm{m^{-2}}$.

---

## 1 — `BCC_glide` vs `BCC_Linear`

| Plane | Mobility | $\rho_f / \rho_i$ | $\varepsilon_p$ (final) |
|-------|----------|-------------------|---------------------------|
| (001) | `BCC_glide` | **1.63** | $1.4\times10^{-9}$ |
| (001) | `BCC_Linear` | 1.29 | $4.2\times10^{-9}$ |
| (111) | `BCC_glide` | **2.13** | $3.6\times10^{-9}$ |
| (111) | `BCC_Linear` | 2.02 | $4.0\times10^{-9}$ |

`BCC_glide` now produces clear loop expansion on both planes; linear drag remains a useful baseline.

## 2 — Frank–Read: setup

- **Source:** single pinned segment ($L\approx500\,\mathrm{nm}$) on (001) or (111).
- **Stress:** uniaxial $\sigma_{zz}=5\times10^8\,\mathrm{Pa}$ (Voigt component 3).
- **Forces:** FMM elasticity on; `BCC_glide` with stiff climb ($MobClimb=10^{-8}$).
- **Goal:** bow-out → loop emission → density multiplication.

---

## 2 — (001): source static in 500 cycles

![(001) Frank–Read: no bow-out yet](../2_frank_read/001_BCC/output/first_vs_final.png)

Density unchanged ($2.3\times10^9\,\mathrm{m^{-2}}$); segment remains straight — (001) orientation / segment length may need longer run or adjusted stress.

---

## 2 — (111): multiplication and bow-out

![(111) Frank–Read: loops emitted](../2_frank_read/111_BCC/output/first_vs_final.png)

Density rises **18×** ($2.9\times10^9\to5.2\times10^{10}\,\mathrm{m^{-2}}$); $\varepsilon_p\approx1.7\times10^{-5}$ — classic Frank–Read activity on (111).

---

## 3 — Strain hardening Cu: setup

- **Reference:** OpenDiS `example_fcc_Cu_15um_1e3` (Bertin et al., MSMSE 2019).
- **Box:** 15 µm FCC Cu; 180-chain initial network.
- **Load:** $\dot\varepsilon=10^3\,\mathrm{s^{-1}}$ along [001]; target 1% total strain.
- **Mobility:** `FCC_0`; Taylor FMM; cross-slip on.

---

## 3 — Cu: rising flow stress

![Cu — stress vs total strain](../3_strain_harden_Cu/output/stress_vs_total_strain.png)

Flow stress increases from $\sim0.03\,\mathrm{MPa}$ to $\sim2\,\mathrm{MPa}$ over the run; density $\approx1.1\times10^{12}\,\mathrm{m^{-2}}$ (near initial level).

---

## 3 — Cu: network morphology

![Cu — initial vs final network](../3_strain_harden_Cu/output/first_vs_final.png)

Dislocation lines rearrange under fast strain-rate loading; full 1% strain target requires completing the `timeEnd` horizon.

---

## 4 — Strain hardening Al: setup

- **Material:** bulk FCC Al (`tests/Al.data`); $\mu=27\,\mathrm{GPa}$, $b=2.86\,\mathrm{\AA}$.
- **Load:** $\dot\varepsilon=10^4\,\mathrm{s^{-1}}$ along [100] (`indxErate = 0`).
- **Mobility:** `FCC_linear`; cross-slip enabled; glide planes not enforced.
- **Forces:** FMM + remote elastic tables (`Rijm` / `RijmPBC`).

---

## 4 — Al: pronounced hardening curve

![Al — stress vs total strain](../4_strain_harden_Al/output/stress_vs_total_strain.png)

Stress rises from $\sim0.04\,\mathrm{MPa}$ to $\sim9\,\mathrm{MPa}$; longer run reaches $\varepsilon_p\approx1.9\times10^{-5}$ with $\rho\approx6.8\times10^{12}\,\mathrm{m^{-2}}$.

---

## 4 — Al: dense network at late time

![Al — initial vs final network](../4_strain_harden_Al/output/first_vs_final.png)

Higher strain rate and initial density produce a busy final tangle — suitable for stress–density diagnostics.

## Summary

1. **`BCC_glide` glissile loops** expand on (001) and (111) after mobility-law fix and per-plane `MobClimb` tuning.
2. **Frank–Read (111)** multiplies dislocations under $\sigma_{zz}$; **(001)** remains static in the current 500-cycle window.
3. **FCC strain-hardening** examples (Cu, Al) show rising flow stress and evolving networks under constant $\dot\varepsilon$.
4. **Shared workflow:** ParaDiS run → `examples/utils/visualize.py` → `output/` figures for all cases above.
